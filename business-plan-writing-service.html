The iWrite Business Plan Process

Our proven methodology transforms your business concept into a compelling, fundable business plan through systematic development and refinement:

  1. Discovery and Strategic Assessment - Comprehensive interviews with founders and key team members to understand business model, goals, and constraints. We analyze market opportunity, competitive landscape, and funding requirements to establish strategic foundation.
  2. Market Research and Competitive Analysis - Thorough market research including industry analysis, target customer profiling, competitive positioning, and market size quantification. We identify market trends and opportunities that support your business thesis.
  3. Financial Modeling and Projections - Development of comprehensive financial models including revenue projections, cost structures, cash flow analysis, and scenario planning. All projections are based on market research and realistic assumptions.
  4. Business Plan Writing and Development - Professional writing of all business plan sections with compelling narratives that tell your story while addressing investor concerns and questions. Every section is optimized for clarity, credibility, and persuasiveness.
  5. Review, Refinement, and Finalization - Multiple review cycles with stakeholder feedback incorporation, fact-checking, and polish. Final formatting and presentation ensure professional appearance that reflects well on your organization.

Professional Financial Modeling and Analysis

The financial section often determines whether your business plan receives serious consideration or immediate rejection. Our business plan writing service includes sophisticated financial modeling that demonstrates thorough understanding of your business economics and realistic pathway to profitability.

Comprehensive Financial Models

Our financial models go beyond simple revenue projections to provide detailed analysis of business drivers, cost structures, and profitability dynamics that investors and lenders require for decision-making.

Revenue Modeling and Forecasting

Bottom-up revenue models based on customer acquisition, pricing strategies, and market penetration rates. Includes sensitivity analysis for key assumptions and multiple scenario planning.

Revenue Model Elements:
  • Customer acquisition and retention rates
  • Pricing strategies and revenue per customer
  • Market penetration and growth rates
  • Seasonality and cyclical factors
  • Geographic and segment expansion

Cost Structure and Operating Model

Detailed cost analysis including fixed and variable cost components, scalability factors, and operational leverage opportunities that demonstrate path to profitability.

Cost Analysis Includes:
  • Cost of goods sold and gross margin analysis
  • Personnel costs and scaling requirements
  • Marketing and customer acquisition costs
  • Technology and infrastructure investments
  • Working capital and inventory requirements

Cash Flow and Working Capital

Monthly cash flow projections with detailed working capital analysis, funding requirements, and cash management strategies that demonstrate financial viability.

Cash Flow Components:
  • Operating cash flow generation
  • Capital expenditure requirements
  • Working capital changes and seasonality
  • Debt service and financing requirements
  • Break-even analysis and cash burn rates

Investor-Grade Financial Statements

Professional financial statements formatted according to GAAP standards with supporting schedules and assumptions that demonstrate financial sophistication and planning rigor.

Profit and Loss Projections

3-5 year monthly and annual P&L statements with detailed revenue and expense line items, gross and operating margin analysis, and profitability progression.

Balance Sheet Projections

Complete balance sheets showing asset requirements, capital structure, and debt-to-equity ratios that support growth plans and financing strategies.

Cash Flow Statements

Operating, investing, and financing cash flows with working capital analysis and free cash flow generation supporting valuation and investment returns.

Valuation and Investment Returns

For equity investors, our business plans include preliminary valuation analysis and return projections that help investors understand potential returns and exit opportunities.

Valuation Methodologies:

  • Comparable Company Analysis: Valuation multiples from similar public and private companies
  • Discounted Cash Flow: DCF models with appropriate discount rates and terminal values
  • Market-Based Approaches: Transaction multiples and market conditions analysis
  • Asset-Based Methods: When appropriate for asset-intensive businesses

Investment Return Analysis:

  • IRR and multiple of money returns for different exit scenarios
  • Sensitivity analysis showing returns under various outcomes
  • Exit strategy analysis including timing and valuation expectations
  • Liquidity preferences and capital structure optimization

What makes your business plans different from templates or DIY approaches?

Our business plans include original market research, custom financial modeling, and professional writing tailored to your specific industry and funding sources. Unlike templates, each plan is built from scratch based on your unique business model and market opportunity.

Do you help with the fundraising process beyond writing the business plan?

Yes, we provide funding strategy consultation, investor introductions through our network, and pitch presentation development. Many clients continue working with us throughout the fundraising process for ongoing support and plan updates.

How detailed are the financial projections in your business plans?

Our financial models include monthly cash flow for the first year, quarterly for years 2-3, and annual for years 4-5. We provide detailed assumptions, sensitivity analysis, and multiple scenarios. All projections are based on market research and realistic business drivers.

Absolutely. We have extensive experience with SBA loan applications and bank financing requirements. Our business plans for traditional lending emphasize cash flow generation, asset security, and conservative projections that banks prefer.

What if my business model is still evolving or I'm not sure about some details?

That's normal for many businesses. Our discovery process helps clarify business model details and strategic decisions. We often help entrepreneurs refine their strategies as part of the business planning process, ensuring the final plan reflects a viable, well-thought-out approach.

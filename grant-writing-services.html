The iWrite Grant Writing Process

Our proven methodology transforms your ideas into fundable proposals through systematic development and refinement:

  1. Discovery and Opportunity Analysis - We conduct comprehensive interviews with key stakeholders to understand your goals, capacity, and constraints. Parallel research identifies optimal funding opportunities with detailed funder analysis.
  2. Strategic Positioning and Narrative Development - Working closely with your team, we develop compelling problem statements and solution frameworks that align with funder priorities while showcasing your unique strengths and competitive advantages.
  3. Technical Writing and Content Creation - Our expert grant writers craft persuasive narratives supported by robust methodology, realistic budgets, and compelling evaluation plans. Every section is optimized for maximum impact and reviewer engagement.
  4. Review and Refinement - Multiple review cycles ensure accuracy, completeness, and competitive positioning. We incorporate feedback from technical experts, program staff, and external reviewers to strengthen every aspect of your proposal.
  5. Final Preparation and Submission Support - We handle all formatting, compliance checking, and submission logistics, ensuring your proposal arrives on time and meets all requirements. Post-submission support includes reviewer feedback analysis and resubmission planning.

What Makes Our Grant Proposals Successful

Successful grant writing requires more than following guidelines—it demands strategic thinking, compelling storytelling, and meticulous attention to detail. Our grant writing services incorporate proven success factors that consistently deliver results.

Strategic Positioning and Competitive Analysis

Every successful grant proposal begins with clear understanding of the competitive landscape and strategic positioning that differentiates your organization and approach.

Our Strategic Approach Includes:

  • Funder Priority Alignment: Deep analysis of program guidelines, recent awards, and funder communications to ensure perfect alignment between your proposal and their priorities.
  • Competitive Differentiation: Research into similar organizations and projects to identify unique positioning and competitive advantages that set your proposal apart.
  • Stakeholder Mapping: Comprehensive analysis of key players, potential partners, and influencers who can strengthen your proposal and implementation success.
  • Risk Assessment: Proactive identification and mitigation of potential concerns or weaknesses that could undermine proposal competitiveness.

Compelling Narrative Development

The most successful grant proposals tell compelling stories that emotionally engage reviewers while providing logical, evidence-based arguments for funding.

Problem Definition and Urgency

We craft problem statements that create emotional investment while demonstrating clear understanding of root causes, affected populations, and consequences of inaction.

Solution Innovation and Evidence

Our proposals present innovative approaches supported by preliminary data, pilot results, and theoretical frameworks that build confidence in success likelihood.

Impact Articulation

Clear, measurable outcomes with realistic timelines and compelling long-term implications that justify investment and create excitement about potential.

Organizational Credibility

Strategic presentation of organizational strengths, track record, and capacity that builds confidence in successful implementation and stewardship.

Technical Excellence and Methodology

Beyond compelling narratives, successful grants require robust methodology, realistic planning, and attention to technical detail that demonstrates competence and preparedness.

  • Research Design and Methodology: Scientifically sound approaches with appropriate controls, sample sizes, and analytical methods that meet disciplinary standards.
  • Project Management and Timelines: Realistic work plans with clear milestones, deliverables, and contingency planning that demonstrate project management competence.
  • Budget Development and Justification: Detailed, accurate budgets with clear cost justifications that maximize value while meeting all compliance requirements.
  • Evaluation and Assessment Plans: Comprehensive evaluation frameworks with appropriate metrics, data collection methods, and analysis plans that demonstrate commitment to accountability.
  • Sustainability Planning: Clear strategies for continuing impact beyond the grant period, including funding diversification and institutionalization plans.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is your actual success rate for grant applications?

Our overall success rate is 85%, compared to the industry average of 10-15% for federal grants and 25-30% for foundation grants. This high success rate reflects our strategic approach, expert writing, and rigorous quality assurance process. We track success rates by funding source and project type to continuously improve our services.

How do you match writers to specific grant opportunities?

We maintain a team of specialized grant writers with expertise in different sectors and funding sources. Each project is assigned based on the writer's subject matter expertise, funder experience, and track record with similar grants. For complex projects, we often use writer teams with complementary expertise.

Can you help with grant management after funding is secured?

Yes, we offer grant management services including reporting, compliance monitoring, and stewardship communications. Many clients continue working with us for the life of their grants to ensure successful implementation and positioning for renewal or continuation funding.

What information do you need from us to get started?

We need basic organizational information, project descriptions, target funding sources, and access to key personnel for interviews. Our discovery process is designed to efficiently gather all necessary information while minimizing time demands on your team. We provide detailed preparation materials to streamline this process.

How do you handle confidential or proprietary information?

We maintain strict confidentiality protocols and sign NDAs as needed. Our team has extensive experience with proprietary research, competitive information, and sensitive organizational data. All project materials are securely stored and access is limited to essential personnel.

What happens if our grant application is not successful?

We provide detailed reviewer feedback analysis and recommendations for improvement. Many unsuccessful applications can be strengthened and resubmitted. We offer discounted rates for resubmissions and have successfully secured funding on second or third attempts for many clients.

Do you work with first-time grant applicants?

Absolutely. We have extensive experience helping organizations secure their first major grants. Our process includes grant readiness assessment and organizational development recommendations to ensure you're well-positioned for success. We also provide training and capacity building to support long-term grant success.

How far in advance should we start the grant writing process?

Ideally, 6-8 weeks before the deadline for complex federal grants, 4-6 weeks for foundation grants. However, we regularly handle rush projects when necessary. Earlier engagement allows for better strategic planning and competitive positioning, but we can adapt our process to meet tight deadlines when needed.
